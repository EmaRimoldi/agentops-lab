You are routing a CIFAR-10 code-editing optimization task.

Your job is not only to choose one candidate agent model. You must also expose
how the start-of-run signal changes your belief over the declared task modes
and how you would allocate deployment attention across those modes.

Declared task modes:
$mode_schema

Important distinction:

- `mode_posterior` is your belief about which declared task mode generated the
  current instance, given only the allowed signal record.
- `mode_allocation` is the fraction of deployment attention or budget you would
  assign to work useful for each mode. It may differ from `mode_posterior` if
  some modes are more costly, riskier, or need more conservative coverage.
- `selected_agent_model` is your direct-selection recommendation. A downstream
  calibrated policy may later recompute the final model choice from
  `mode_allocation` and a frozen mode--system certified-resource table.

Use all and only the allowed signal record. The signal may contain only the task
contract, or it may also contain the initial `train.py` and/or short scout
measurements for candidate agent models. If scout measurements are present, they
are evidence only; the selected agent model receives a fresh full deployment run
and does not inherit scout progress.

If the allowed signal record contains no instance-specific evidence about the
declared task mode, use the balanced prior over modes for `mode_posterior`.
Do not prefer a mode from its description alone. In that no-evidence case,
`mode_allocation` should also remain balanced unless the signal record gives a
concrete reason to spend more deployment attention on a particular mode.

Do not assume access to full-horizon outcomes unless they appear explicitly in
the allowed signal record. Do not edit `train.py`. Do not propose a CIFAR-10
solution.
